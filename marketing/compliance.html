Compliance & Certifications

Last Updated: January 1, 2026

OrenGen.io maintains the highest standards of compliance and security certifications to protect your data and ensure regulatory adherence.

Our Commitment: We are SOC2 Type II certified, GDPR compliant, ISO 27001 certified, and adhere to industry-leading security and privacy standards.

1. Security Certifications

1.1 SOC 2 Type II

Service Organization Control 2 (Type II)

  • Certification Date: December 2025 (annual audit)
  • Auditor: Deloitte & Touche LLP
  • Scope: All AI services, infrastructure, and data processing
  • Trust Service Criteria: Security, Availability, Confidentiality

SOC 2 Type II demonstrates that our controls are not only properly designed but also operating effectively over time.

1.2 ISO 27001:2022

Information Security Management System (ISMS)

  • Certification Date: November 2025
  • Certifying Body: BSI Group
  • Scope: Design, development, and delivery of AI solutions
  • Certificate Number: ISO-27001-2025-OG-001

1.3 ISO 27018

Code of Practice for Cloud Privacy

  • Protection of Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in cloud environments
  • Enhanced transparency and accountability
  • Customer data isolation and segregation

2. Data Privacy Compliance

2.1 GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation)

EU Data Protection Compliance

  • Data Protection Officer: Sarah Johnson (dpo@orengen.io)
  • Legal Basis: Consent, Contract, Legitimate Interest
  • Data Processing Agreements: Available for all EU customers
  • Standard Contractual Clauses: Implemented for data transfers

2.2 CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)

California Resident Rights

  • Right to know what personal data is collected
  • Right to delete personal data
  • Right to opt-out of data sales (we don't sell data)
  • Right to non-discrimination

CCPA Contact: privacy@orengen.io

2.3 HIPAA (Healthcare)

For Healthcare Customers

  • Business Associate Agreements (BAA) available
  • HIPAA-compliant infrastructure and processes
  • PHI (Protected Health Information) encryption and access controls
  • Regular HIPAA security risk assessments

3. Industry-Specific Compliance

3.1 Financial Services

  • PCI DSS: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard compliance for payment processing
  • SOX: Sarbanes-Oxley controls for publicly traded customers
  • GLBA: Gramm-Leach-Bliley Act compliance for financial data

3.2 Telecommunications

  • TCPA: Telephone Consumer Protection Act compliance
  • TRACED Act: Robocall mitigation and STIR/SHAKEN implementation
  • FCC Regulations: Adherence to Federal Communications Commission rules

3.3 Education

  • FERPA: Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act
  • COPPA: Children's Online Privacy Protection Act
  • Student Data Privacy: State-specific student privacy laws

4. Accessibility Compliance

4.1 WCAG 2.1 Level AA

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

  • Perceivable: Content is available to all senses
  • Operable: Interface is navigable by all users
  • Understandable: Information is clear and consistent
  • Robust: Compatible with assistive technologies

4.2 ADA Compliance

Americans with Disabilities Act

  • Website accessibility for screen readers
  • Keyboard navigation support
  • Alternative text for images
  • Accessible forms and error handling

5. Export Compliance

5.1 EAR & ITAR

  • Export Administration Regulations (EAR): Compliance with U.S. export controls
  • ITAR: International Traffic in Arms Regulations (for defense customers)
  • Denied Parties Screening: Regular screening against restricted party lists

5.2 Economic Sanctions

  • OFAC sanctions compliance
  • Restricted countries and entities screening
  • Regular monitoring and updates

6. AI & ML Compliance

6.1 AI Ethics & Governance

  • AI Principles: Fairness, transparency, accountability, privacy
  • Bias Mitigation: Regular audits for algorithmic bias
  • Explainable AI: Transparent decision-making processes
  • Human Oversight: Human-in-the-loop for critical decisions

6.2 Emerging AI Regulations

  • EU AI Act: Monitoring and preparing for compliance
  • Algorithmic Accountability: Documentation and testing procedures
  • Data Minimization: Only collecting necessary data for AI training

7. Business Compliance

7.1 Corporate Governance

  • Business Type: Delaware C-Corporation
  • Registration: All required state and federal registrations current
  • Insurance: Cyber liability, E&O, general liability coverage

7.2 Certifications & Memberships

  • HUB Certified: Historically Underutilized Business
  • Minority Business Enterprise: Certified by NMSDC
  • Industry Associations: Member of AICPA, Cloud Security Alliance, IAPP

8. Vendor & Supply Chain Management

8.1 Third-Party Risk Management

  • Comprehensive vendor security assessments
  • Annual audits of critical vendors
  • Data processing agreements with all sub-processors
  • Right-to-audit clauses in vendor contracts

8.2 Approved Sub-Processors

We maintain a list of all sub-processors who may process customer data:

  • Cloud Infrastructure: AWS, Google Cloud, Azure
  • Analytics: Google Analytics, Mixpanel
  • Customer Support: Zendesk, Intercom
  • Payment Processing: Stripe, PayPal

Full sub-processor list available at: orengen.io/sub-processors

9. Audit & Reporting

9.1 Internal Audits

  • Quarterly: Security control effectiveness reviews
  • Annually: Comprehensive SOC 2 and ISO 27001 audits
  • Continuous: Automated compliance monitoring

9.2 Customer Audit Rights

Enterprise customers may request:

  • SOC 2 Type II reports (under NDA)
  • ISO 27001 certificates
  • Security questionnaires and assessments
  • On-site audits (subject to availability and terms)

9.3 Transparency Reporting

We publish annual transparency reports covering:

  • Government data requests
  • Security incidents (if any)
  • Service availability statistics
  • Privacy inquiries and responses

10. Breach Notification

10.1 Incident Response

In the event of a data breach, we will:

  • Notification Timeline: Within 72 hours of discovery (GDPR requirement)
  • Affected Parties: Notify all impacted individuals and customers
  • Regulatory Reporting: Report to relevant authorities as required
  • Remediation: Immediate action to contain and resolve the breach

10.2 Security Incident History

As of January 1, 2026: OrenGen.io has experienced zero data breaches or security incidents resulting in unauthorized access to customer data.
